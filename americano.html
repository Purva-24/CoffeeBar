Americano

An americano is a coffee drink made by diluting a shot of espresso with hot water, typically in a ratio of 1:3 to 1:4, resulting in a beverage with a strength similar to traditional drip coffee but with a distinct flavor profile derived from the espresso.

The preparation method can vary, with some baristas adding the espresso to hot water first to preserve the crema, while others may reverse the order, though the latter is more common in the preparation of a long black, a similar but distinct drink popular in Australasia.
